Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 2, 2026 Last updated: May 2, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how SuitesForAll (“SuitesForAll,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, shares, and protects personal information when you use our property-management web application available at https://suitesforall.web.app (the “Service”).

This policy applies to two groups of users:

  • Operators — landlords, property managers, and their team members who sign in to manage buildings, units, leases, billing, and tenants.
  • Tenants — individuals or businesses who rent units from an Operator and whose information the Operator enters into the Service for billing, lease management, and communication.
Quick summary. We collect what an Operator needs to manage their properties and what tenants need to pay rent. We do not sell personal information. Payments are processed by Stripe; e-signatures by DocuSign; bank-account linking (when used) by Stripe Financial Connections. You have the right to access, correct, delete, and export your data — see Section 9.

1. Who we are

SuitesForAll is operated by Al-En, a property-management technology business based in the United States. For privacy-related inquiries, the data controller / business contact is:

  • Email: tony@al-en.com
  • Service URL: https://suitesforall.web.app

If you are a tenant, your landlord (the Operator who entered your information into the Service) is the primary data controller for your tenancy data; SuitesForAll acts as a data processor on the Operator’s behalf for that data, and as data controller for our own technical and account-administration data.

2. Information we collect

a) Operator account information

  • Name, email address, phone number, and the workspace role assigned to you (admin, manager, map editor, viewer).
  • Authentication identifiers (Firebase user ID, sign-in timestamps, IP address used for sign-in).
  • Workspace settings, building and floor configuration, uploaded floor-plan images and PDFs.

b) Tenant information (entered by Operators)

  • Tenant name, company name, email address, phone number.
  • Lease terms: start date, end date, suite number, rent amount, deposit, executed-lease PDF (when DocuSign is used).
  • Billing data: invoice amounts, payment status, payment dates, late-fee history, outreach notes.
  • Optional notes and attachments stored against the tenancy record.

c) Payment information

When an Operator sends an invoice or a tenant pays online, payment-method information (card number, bank account details, billing address) is collected and processed directly by Stripe. SuitesForAll never receives, stores, or transmits raw card numbers or full bank-account credentials. We receive from Stripe only metadata such as the Stripe customer ID, invoice ID, payment status, last four digits, and brand/network.

d) Bank-feed information (optional, Operator-only)

If an Operator chooses to connect a business bank account to the Service via Stripe Financial Connections, we receive (subject to the Operator’s consent at the time of linking): the financial institution name, masked account number (last four), account type, account holder name, current and available balances, and a feed of incoming and outgoing transactions. This data is used solely to match incoming deposits to outstanding rent invoices. See Section 6 for details.

e) Communications

  • Emails sent through the Service (invoices, reminders, lease documents) and their metadata (subject, recipient, send timestamp, delivery status).
  • Tenant outreach log entries (calls, emails, notes) recorded by the Operator.

f) Technical and usage information

  • Browser type, operating system, screen resolution, and language.
  • IP address and approximate location (city/region) derived from it.
  • Pages and features accessed, timestamps of actions, error events.
  • Locally stored data in your browser (see Section 13).

g) Optional integrations

If an Operator connects optional services (Gmail, DocuSign, Stripe, OpenRouter / OpenAI, UniFi Access), we receive and store the OAuth tokens or API keys necessary to call those services on the Operator’s behalf. We never share these credentials with third parties.

3. How we collect it

  • Directly from you — when you create an account, sign in, enter information into the Service, upload a file, or contact support.
  • From Operators about Tenants — when an Operator creates a tenancy record on your behalf.
  • Automatically — when you use the Service, your browser sends technical metadata (IP, headers, user-agent) and the Service stores local-state data in your browser’s localStorage and IndexedDB.
  • From third-party integrations you connect — Stripe (customers, invoices, charges, Financial Connections accounts and transactions), DocuSign (envelope status), Gmail (sent-message metadata), OpenRouter / OpenAI (model responses to your prompts), UniFi Access (access-control events).

4. How we use information

We use the information described above to:

  • Provide, operate, secure, and maintain the Service.
  • Authenticate Operators and enforce role-based permissions.
  • Generate, send, and reconcile rent invoices and other charges.
  • Match incoming bank deposits to outstanding invoices (when Bank Feed is enabled).
  • Send transactional communications (invoices, reminders, lease documents, receipts).
  • Render floor plans, including AI-assisted room detection from uploaded floor-plan images when an Operator chooses to use that feature.
  • Maintain audit logs for security, debugging, and compliance.
  • Take periodic backups of workspace data so it can be restored if accidentally deleted.
  • Respond to your support requests and improve the Service.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms.

We do not use personal information for advertising, profiling unrelated to the Service, or sale to third parties.

6. Bank-account connections (Stripe Financial Connections)

If you are an Operator and you choose to connect a business bank account to the Service, the linking flow is operated by Stripe Financial Connections. When you click Connect bank account in Settings → Integrations → Bank Connections:

  1. Stripe’s widget opens in your browser.
  2. You select your financial institution and authenticate using your bank’s own login.
  3. Your bank shows you exactly which permissions you are granting (transactions, balances, account ownership).
  4. On success, Stripe issues SuitesForAll a token that lets us read transactions and balances for the linked account(s) only.

What we do with the data:

  • Periodically poll the connected account for new transactions (typically every several hours).
  • Match incoming deposits to outstanding rent invoices in your workspace.
  • Display the matched payment status and (optionally) the current account balance to authorized members of your workspace.

What we do not do:

  • We do not initiate withdrawals, transfers, or any movement of money out of your account through this connection.
  • We do not share the bank-feed data with anyone outside your workspace and Stripe.
  • We do not use the data for credit decisioning, marketing, or any purpose unrelated to invoice reconciliation.

Your rights regarding the bank connection: you may revoke access at any time by clicking Disconnect on the connected account in Settings → Integrations → Bank Connections. You may also revoke directly from your bank’s online banking portal or from your Stripe dashboard. Revocation stops all future data retrieval; data already pulled is retained according to Section 8.

Stripe’s end-user disclosures for Financial Connections (which describe Stripe’s independent role and obligations) are available at stripe.com/legal/end-users.

7. Legal bases for processing (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, our legal bases for processing personal information are:

  • Performance of a contract — to provide the Service to Operators under our terms, and to enable Operators to fulfil their lease contracts with Tenants.
  • Legitimate interests — to operate, secure, and improve the Service; to prevent fraud and abuse; to debug; to maintain audit logs.
  • Consent — for optional features such as bank-account linking, Gmail integration, and AI-assisted floor-plan detection. You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legal obligation — to comply with tax, accounting, and other applicable laws.

8. Data retention

We retain personal information for as long as is necessary to provide the Service and to comply with legal obligations. In practice:

  • Operator account data — for the lifetime of the workspace, plus 90 days after deletion (to allow restoration from backups).
  • Tenant records — at the Operator’s discretion, typically for the duration of the tenancy plus a retention window required by local landlord-tenant and tax law (often 7 years in the US).
  • Payment records — retained for at least 7 years to satisfy financial-record-keeping obligations.
  • Bank-feed transactions — retained for the active period of the bank connection plus 24 months, after which they are aggregated or deleted.
  • Backups — daily snapshots are kept for at least 30 days; manual backups for at least 90 days.
  • Audit logs — retained for at least 12 months.

An Operator may request earlier deletion of specific tenant records via the in-app Erase Tenant PII action or by contacting us at the email below.

12. Children’s privacy

The Service is not directed to children under the age of 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it.

13. Cookies and local storage

The Service uses your browser’s localStorage and IndexedDB (not classic HTTP cookies) to store your sign-in state, your last-used workspace settings, draft form data, and integration credentials you have voluntarily entered. Firebase Authentication uses cookies set by Google for the OAuth and session-management flow.

We do not use third-party advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics that profile individual users for marketing purposes. You can clear local browser storage at any time via your browser’s settings; doing so will sign you out of the Service.

14. Security

We apply appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal information, including:

  • HTTPS / TLS encryption for all data in transit.
  • Encryption at rest in Google Cloud infrastructure.
  • Role-based access control inside each workspace; principle of least privilege.
  • API secrets stored exclusively in Google Cloud Secret Manager and never embedded in client code.
  • Stripe webhook signature verification on every inbound event.
  • Daily encrypted backups with retention.
  • Audit logging of sensitive actions (deploys, deletions, financial events).

No system is perfectly secure. If you become aware of a security issue, please report it to tony@al-en.com.

SuitesForAll’s use of information received from Google APIs (specifically the Gmail API, when an Operator connects their Gmail account) adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. In particular:

  • We use Gmail data only to provide user-facing features (sending invoices, reminders, and lease documents) that are prominently visible inside the Service.
  • We do not transfer Gmail data to third parties except as necessary to provide or improve user-facing features, comply with applicable law, or as part of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets — all subject to user consent.
