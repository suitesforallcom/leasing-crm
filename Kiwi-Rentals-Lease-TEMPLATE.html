Office Services Agreement

Kiwi Rentals LLC — Commercial Workspace License

This Office Services Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into as of [lease_start]  (“Effective Date”), by and between KIWI RENTALS LLC, a Delaware limited liability company with its principal place of business at 108 West 13th Street, Wilmington, Delaware (“Provider”), and [client_company]  (“Client”).

1. Premises

Provider grants Client a revocable, non-exclusive license to use Suite [suite]  (the “Space”) located at [building_address]  (the “Building”), together with shared common areas and the services listed in Section 4. This is a license for temporary, non-exclusive use — not a lease. Provider retains full possession and control of the Building.

2. Term

The term of this Agreement begins on [lease_start]  and ends on [lease_end]  (initial term of [term] ). The Agreement renews automatically for successive terms of equal length unless either party delivers at least sixty (60) days’ written notice of non-renewal.

3. Fees & Deposit

Financial Terms

Monthly Base Fee $[rent]  payable in advance on the 1st of each month
Security Deposit $[deposit]  held and refundable per Section 6
Late Fee 5% of the unpaid Base Fee if not received by the 5th of the month
NSF / Returned Payment $35 per occurrence, plus late fee if applicable

Client authorizes Provider to invoice electronically and may, at Client’s option, set up automatic payment from a card or bank on file. Partial months prorate on a 30-day basis. Taxes and governmental charges attributable to the Space or services are the responsibility of Client.

4. Services Included

Provider will furnish, at no additional charge: shared Wi-Fi internet (best-effort, no uptime guarantee), climate control during posted business hours (Mon–Fri, excluding federal holidays), routine cleaning of common areas, electricity, water, and basic reception during business hours. Services not listed are available at then-current rates on request.

5. Client Obligations

Client shall: (a) pay all Fees when due; (b) use the Space only for lawful general office purposes; (c) comply with House Rules, as reasonably updated by Provider with notice; (d) not assign, sublicense, or share the Space without Provider’s prior written consent; (e) keep the Space in clean, undamaged condition and return it in the same state at termination, ordinary wear excepted; (f) maintain Commercial General Liability insurance of at least $1,000,000 per occurrence and name Provider as an additional insured on request.

6. Security Deposit

The deposit set forth in Section 3 is held as security for performance. Provider may apply it against unpaid Fees, damage beyond ordinary wear, cleaning or restoration costs, or other amounts owed. Any remaining balance is refunded within thirty (30) days after the later of termination or Client’s vacating of the Space.

7. Termination

By Client: Client may terminate early with ninety (90) days’ written notice, paying an Early Termination Fee equal to the lesser of three months’ Base Fee or the remaining term’s Base Fees, plus any unpaid charges.
By Provider: Provider may terminate with written notice for non-payment, uncured material breach after ten (10) days’ notice, insolvency of Client, safety or legal violations, or if Provider ceases operating the Building.

8. Liability & Indemnity

Provider is not liable for loss of business, lost profits, or consequential damages. Except for injury or death caused by a party’s gross negligence or willful misconduct, each party’s aggregate liability is capped at the total Base Fees paid by Client in the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim. Client shall indemnify and hold Provider harmless from third-party claims arising out of Client’s use of the Space or breach of this Agreement.

9. Governing Law & Disputes

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Florida, without regard to conflict-of-laws rules. Any dispute shall first be subject to good-faith negotiation and, if unresolved, resolved by binding arbitration in Pinellas County, Florida, under the Commercial Arbitration Rules of the American Arbitration Association, before one arbitrator. Either party may seek temporary injunctive relief in court pending arbitration. No class or representative actions.

10. Entire Agreement

This document constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions or understandings. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties. If any provision is held invalid, the remainder continues in full force.
